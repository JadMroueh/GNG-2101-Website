From setup to finished cut

The teacher prepares the station in under a minute. Then the child cuts on her own. Here is the full sequence. The demo on the home page shows it in motion.

Teacher setup

  1. Set the angle

    The teacher tilts the wooden board to the angle that suits the child and locks it in place. The locked angle brings the paper toward her hands.

  2. Load the paper

    The paper lays flat on the galvanized steel plate. Four magnetic strips press it down, one on each side. The sheet stays put without anyone holding it.

  3. Put on the brace

    The brace is a closed loop with an opening through the middle. The child's dominant hand slides through the opening and the teacher straps it snug. The ceramic blade points down, away from fingers.

The child cuts

  1. Place the hand

    She rests her braced hand on the paper where the cut should start. No grip needed. The brace holds the blade for her.

  2. Drag forward

    She drags her hand forward. The blade cuts as it moves. There is no button and no trigger. The motion is the whole control.

  3. The board holds still

    Rubber feet under the board grip the table. The station does not slide while she pushes.

  4. Done

    The magnets release the cut pieces when the teacher lifts the strips. The paper is cut. She did it herself.
