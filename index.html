Cut paper with one hand. No help needed.

The one-hand adaptive cutting station lets a child with limited hand mobility cut paper on her own. A teacher sets it up once. The child does the rest.

Who it is for

Our client is an elementary school student with limited hand mobility. Her dominant hand can push and drag but cannot grip well. Her other arm can push and rotate but cannot grip. She wants to cut paper in class without an adult holding the sheet or working a tool for her.

The station removes every step that needs a grip. Nothing to squeeze. Nothing to hold down. She pushes, drags, and cuts.

Four subsystems, one job

  • Adjustable angled board

    The teacher sets the angle and locks it. The work surface tilts toward the child instead of making her reach.

  • Magnet attachment system

    The paper lays flat on a steel plate. Four magnetic strips hold it down, one on each side, so no hand has to.

  • Wearable cutting brace

    A 3D printed closed loop with an opening for her hand. It holds a ceramic blade facing down. Drag forward to cut. No button. No trigger.

  • Rubber feet stabilizer

    Rubber feet under the board stop it from sliding during the cut.
