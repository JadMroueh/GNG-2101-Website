Safety came first

This is a cutting tool for an elementary school classroom. Safety outranked every other goal, and four layers of the design carry it.

A blade designed not to cut skin

The station uses a Slice 10408 ceramic blade. Slice markets these blades as finger friendly. The edge cuts paper but resists cutting skin at the pressures a child produces. There is no exposed steel edge anywhere on the station.

The blade always points down

The brace holds the blade facing downward, into the paper and the board behind it. Her fingers sit above the blade, inside the brace. The cutting edge never faces her hand, her other arm, or a classmate.

The board locks before she starts

The teacher sets the angle and locks the board before the child touches it. The angle cannot shift mid-cut. The paper is already secured by the magnets when the board raises. Nothing moves that is not supposed to move.

The station stays put

Rubber feet under the board grip the table. When she pushes forward through the cut, the station pushes back. It does not slide toward the edge of the desk or into her lap.

The adult controls the setup

The teacher sets the angle, loads the paper, and straps on the brace. The child never handles the blade directly. The parts she touches are the paper and the outside of the brace. Independence in the cut, supervision in the setup.
